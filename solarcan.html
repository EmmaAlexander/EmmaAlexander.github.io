The solarcan is a type of pinhole camera, also known as a camera obscura. Inside the can, there is a piece of photographic paper that reacts to ultraviolet light. When the Sun is in different parts of the sky, its light falls on different parts of the paper. The paper reacts to the light and turns dark. This means that the solarcan tracks the path of the Sun each day, lower in the winter and higher in the summer. The result is a combined image of every sunny day that the solarcan has seen.
